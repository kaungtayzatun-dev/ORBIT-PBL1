Carbon Emissions

Causes

High traffic volume and idling vehicles lead to excessive burning of fossil fuels.

Consequences

Significant contribution to the greenhouse effect, global warming, and climate instability.

Air Pollution

Causes

Exhaust fumes release particulate matter, nitrogen oxides, and other toxic gases.

Consequences

Widespread respiratory issues, heart disease, and reduced visibility in urban centers.

← Back to Problems

Commuter Stress

Causes

Unpredictable traffic, loud surroundings, and the loss of personal time while sitting in gridlocked traffic.

Consequences

Increased cortisol levels, which lead to fatigue, anxiety, and lowered immune system function.

← Back to Problems

Emergency Delays

Causes

Road blockages and lack of "smart" signal priority make it impossible for emergency vehicles to bypass traffic.

Consequences

Critical delays in life-saving care, often leading to higher mortality rates in emergency situations.

← Back to Problems

Economic Waste

Causes

Inefficient routing, wasted fuel during idling, and loss of man-hours as employees sit in traffic.

Consequences

Massive financial losses for cities and increased operational costs for logistics and delivery services.

← Back to Problems

Urban Noise

Causes

Constant engine revving, honking, and the physical interaction of tires on road surfaces.

Consequences

High noise levels are linked to chronic sleep deprivation, hypertension, and cognitive development delays in children.
